# Design Context

## Target Audience

Visitors are app-store users, collaborators, technical peers, and curious people landing on Inceptyon Labs after seeing one of the studio's public projects. They should quickly understand that this is a small independent studio with real shipped products and a distinct point of view.

## Use Cases

- Understand what Inceptyon Labs makes.
- Move from the studio site to a specific project, store listing, or public website.
- Sense a cohesive studio identity across unrelated apps, games, and tools.
- Contact the studio.

## Brand Personality

Inceptyon Labs should feel like an independent systems archive: precise, cinematic, austere, curious, and a little classified. The work is presented as field systems, dossiers, devices, protocols, and signals. The vibe may be adjacent to serious speculative engineering, but never explicitly references any outside film, franchise, or director.

## Visual Direction

Use graphite black, cold steel, bone white, sparse tungsten accents, hard-edged panels, schematic lines, orbital geometry, dossier surfaces, and analog-meets-digital image treatment. Avoid neon, cyberpunk glow, purple-blue gradients, beige warmth, generic SaaS card grids, and decorative gloss.

## Copy Direction

Copy should be short, concrete, and restrained. Use phrases like "Systems for impossible ideas," "Archive / independent systems lab," "Field Systems," "Enter the archive," "Send a signal," "Open dossier," and "Named, indexed, tested, and put to work."

## Canonical Projects

- Unwyned: deployed wine discovery app with website, iOS, and Android surfaces.
- INCEPTYON: science game deployed on app stores.
- Sequentyol: public sequence exploration system.
- GigaBrain: experimental knowledge and thinking surface.
- Insomnia: monitoring and agent watch surface.

Dreamheist should not appear in the landing-site project set.
